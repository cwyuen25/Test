商業模式及投入市場時間 (審批比重: 30%)

Problem and value proposition
Tripolr is an AI-assisted collaborative travel-planning platform that helps groups turn scattered travel ideas and different preferences into one practical, editable itinerary.

Group travellers commonly move between social media, maps, messaging applications, notes, spreadsheets, and booking websites. Places are saved in different formats, decisions are discussed repeatedly, and manually selected attractions may not form a realistic route. Tripolr brings place collection, group collaboration, itinerary generation, and trip editing into one shared workflow.

Beachhead target market
Tripolr's initial beachhead will be Hong Kong university students planning independent outbound group trips with friends. This segment is suitable for early validation because group members frequently discover places through social media, plan collaboratively, and need to balance different interests, schedules, and limited budgets.

Hong Kong families planning outbound trips will be the second validation segment. Families face similar coordination problems but often require additional consideration of travel pace, children, older family members, accessibility, and schedule reliability. The product will first establish a simple collaboration workflow with student groups before extending testing to family use cases.

Business model
Tripolr's core planning and collaboration functions will remain free during the validation stage to reduce adoption barriers and encourage multi-user participation. The first commercial hypothesis is that users who have completed a collaborative itinerary demonstrate stronger booking intent and may convert to relevant accommodation, transport, attraction, activity, or travel-insurance referrals.

The planned commercial sequence is:
Phase 1 - CCMF validation: free planning and collaboration, with measurement of usage, retention, collaboration, and booking interest.
Phase 2 - Referral validation: affiliate or referral links for selected high-intent travel categories.
Phase 3 - Commercial integration: direct partnerships and deeper booking integrations after user demand has been demonstrated.
Phase 4 - Additional opportunities: clearly labelled sponsored placements and separately validated B2B group-planning solutions.

Any sponsored content or paid recommendation will be clearly identified. Commercial relationships will not be presented as independent AI recommendations.

Go-to-market strategy

The first pilot will prioritise Hong Kong university student groups. Family testing will follow after the core workflow has been simplified. Potential partnerships with student organisations, travel content creators, travel communities, and service providers will be explored after the pilot provides evidence of user needs and engagement.

Key risks and mitigation
Third-party platform dependency: Social-platform input methods may change. Tripolr will avoid making the whole product dependent on one platform and will support alternative link or manual place-entry methods.

Information accuracy: Opening hours, prices, availability, and entry requirements may change. Recommendations will show appropriate cautions and encourage users to verify important details.

AI output quality: AI suggestions may be incomplete or unsuitable. Users will remain able to review, edit, accept, or reject recommendations. Testing and feedback will be used to improve prompts, rules, and quality checks.

API and cloud costs: Usage and cost will be monitored during the pilot. Expensive operations will be limited, cached, or redesigned where appropriate.

Founder capacity: The founder will retain ownership of priorities and acceptance testing, while clearly scoped specialist tasks may be outsourced to protect the six-month timeline.


所建議項目、產品及服務的創意及創新元素 (審批比重: 30%)

Tripolr's innovation is not limited to asking AI to write an itinerary. Its objective is to connect the full group-planning workflow: convert fragmented travel inspiration into structured places, collect the preferences of multiple travellers, arrange selected places under practical constraints, and help the group adapt the plan when circumstances change.

1. Social inspiration to structured trip data
Travellers often discover places through Instagram and Threads, but saved posts, screenshots, and shared links are difficult to organise. Tripolr extracts relevant place information from supported social content and places it directly into a shared trip. Users can then review and organise the extracted places instead of searching for every location again.

This capability is available in the current prototype and creates a direct connection between travel discovery and planning.

2. Collaborative preference reconciliation
Most generic AI travel tools respond to one prompt or one user. Tripolr is designed around a shared trip containing contributions from multiple travellers.

During CCMF, Tripolr will strengthen structured preference collection so group members can indicate interests, budgets, travel pace, scheduling constraints, and preferred places. The system will help identify common choices and conflicts and support the group in producing a balanced plan. Users will remain in control and may edit or reject proposed compromises.

3. Constraint-aware itinerary construction
A list of attractive places is not necessarily a workable itinerary. Tripolr will combine selected places with practical constraints such as location, estimated travel time, available trip duration, user preferences, and place information. Nearby places can be grouped together, while unrealistic combinations can be highlighted or reorganised.

The aim is to combine AI-assisted recommendations with explicit travel constraints rather than return only generic destination text.

4. Editable and partial replanning
Travel plans change because of delays, availability, weather, schedule changes, or new group decisions. Regenerating the entire itinerary can destroy choices that the group has already accepted.

Tripolr will develop flexible editing and partial replanning so the affected part of an itinerary can be adjusted while preserving as much of the accepted plan as possible. Alternatives can be filtered using available time, location, budget, and group preferences.

5. Human-controlled planning workflow
Tripolr will distinguish between extracted information, AI-generated suggestions, and user-confirmed decisions. Users will review and edit their itineraries, and important travel details will not be presented as guaranteed facts. This human-controlled approach is intended to make AI useful for group decisions without removing user judgement.

Technical approach
The intended planning workflow consists of:
1. User input through social links, saved places, or manual entry;
2. Extraction and normalisation of place information;
3. Location matching and information checks where supported;
4. Collection of trip constraints and group preferences;
5. AI-assisted recommendation and itinerary construction;
6. Route and travel-time checks;
7. User review, collaboration, and editing; and
8. Partial replanning when requirements change.

The product may combine deterministic rules, data from third-party services, optimisation logic, and AI-generated suggestions. The project will test which method is most reliable for each decision rather than use AI for every operation.

Competitive positioning
Tripolr is positioned as a free collaborative planning layer between travel inspiration and booking. Manual combinations such as messaging applications, maps, and spreadsheets support discussion but require users to organise information themselves. General AI assistants can generate itinerary text but are not designed as a persistent shared trip workspace. Booking-led applications support transactions but may not focus on reconciling group preferences before booking.

Tripolr's intended differentiation is the connected workflow from social discovery to structured places, multi-user decision-making, route-aware itinerary editing, and adaptive replanning. The CCMF project will validate whether this combination produces sufficient user value and repeat behaviour.


社會責任 (審批比重: 10%)

Tripolr will focus its social-responsibility commitments on free core access, responsible AI, privacy, and inclusive planning.

1. Free access to core planning
Core place collection, itinerary planning, and collaboration will remain free during the validation stage. This lowers the barrier for university students, families, and budget-conscious travellers to use structured planning tools without a subscription.

2. Responsible and transparent AI
AI-generated outputs will be presented as suggestions rather than guaranteed facts. Users will be able to review, edit, accept, or reject recommendations. Tripolr will encourage verification of important details such as opening hours, prices, availability, transport arrangements, and entry requirements.

Tripolr will distinguish sponsored or paid content from independent planning suggestions. Commercial relationships will be clearly labelled to preserve user trust.

3. Privacy and permission controls
Collaborative travel planning can involve preferences, locations, schedules, and other personal information. Tripolr will collect only information needed to provide the service, implement appropriate access controls, and give users control over who can view or edit a shared trip. Privacy and data-handling requirements will be reviewed as part of the CCMF development plan.

4. Inclusive and practical planning
Where users provide relevant needs, the planning workflow will consider budget, travel pace, available time, and accessibility requirements. This can support groups with different ages, mobility needs, or scheduling constraints. Recommendations will remain subject to user review.

5. More efficient routes
By grouping nearby places and considering travel time, Tripolr aims to reduce unnecessary movement, avoid unrealistic schedules, and help users make more efficient use of time and transport. Environmental or cost benefits will be treated as potential outcomes and will not be claimed without supporting measurement.

Through these commitments, Tripolr aims to make collaborative travel planning more accessible and transparent while maintaining human control over AI-assisted decisions.
